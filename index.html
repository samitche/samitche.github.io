Research

My current research explores the synergies from geographic clustering (or co-location) of literary artists in historical UK and Ireland, combining elements from cultural economics, urban economics and economic history.

My doctoral research is centered around a unique, purpose-built dataset on 370 authors associated with British and Celtic literature since 1700. My initial analysis involved tracking patterns of individual migration, identifying the cities in which authors tend to cluster (or co-locate) and es- timating the productivity gains associated with this literary agglomeration. In my next paper, I empirically determine the local determinants of these agglomeration effects, focusing on mechanisms theoretically associated with learning-based agglomeration economies. In my final paper, I propose social network analysis as a new, useful and relatively unexplored channel of tacit knowledge exchange (i.e. a mechanism through which agglomeration gains occur.) To complete this analysis, I collected and analysed additional data on friendship networks and membership in literary societies and combined this with data on the spatial distribution of authors.

Working Papers

London Calling? Agglomeration Economies in Literature since 1700 (Job Market Paper - pdf)

This paper utilises a unique, purpose-built panel dataset on prominent authors in the UK and Ireland born 1700â€“1925 to estimate the productivity gains associated with agglomeration of an industry with few capital requirements and no apparent need to cluster geographically. We find the average author experiences productivity gains of 10.68% per annum when re- siding in London, controlling for spatial sorting by skill level. While we do not find evidence of dynamic learning effects, we confirm that our results are not driven by a dynamic spatial self-selection process or authors who strategically build a portfolio of works before moving to London and publishing in quick succession.

PROFESSIONAL

I received a BA in Economics from Hendrix College in 2010, where I was a Miller Center Service Scholar. I received a MPhil in International Peace Studies from TCD in 2011 and a MSc in Economics from TCD in 2012. I am currently a PhD candidate at Trinity College Dublin under the supervision of Prof. John O'Hagan. My main research interests are urban economics, economic history, and cultural economics. I was also one of three young researchers in Ireland selected to attend the 5th Lindau Nobel Laureate Meeting of Economic Sciences in 2015.
